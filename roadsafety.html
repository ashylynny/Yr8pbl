Road Safety All Around The World

Yangon is getting more polluted day by day and its transportation system is one of the factors. Not only that but the transport system is currently inefficient with not enough cycling lanes or walking spaces. Here are the solutions from other countries so Myanmar can cope the issues.

Cycling-friendly cities like Copenhagen.

  • Around 19% bike usage
  • Smart traffic lights for cyclists

Netherlands

Leader in cycling infrastructure and safety.

  • 27% of trips by bicycle
  • Over 35,000 km of bike lanes

Japan

Advanced trains and strict safety culture.

  • Millions use trains daily
  • Extremely low accident rates

Singapore

Highly organised and safe transport system.

  • Very low road death rate
  • Strong public transport network

How will these help Myanmar?

Myanmar can improve road safety by building more cycle lanes, improving public transport, and introducing smarter traffic systems. Using ideas from countries like the Netherlands and Singapore can reduce accidents and make cities cleaner and easier to travel in.

Current road safety related issues in Myanmar

  • Hazardous pedestrian condition lead to frequent accidents amoung citizens when walking on pavements in the city. There's a need for well-maintaince, safety and functionally structured layouts among sidewalks.
  • Poor traffic management and intersections causes confusion due to its diagonal and disorganized changes. Traffic lights are set at weird angles and postions which often causes misunderstandings. Sometimes, traffic lights won't even function due to unpredictable electricity.
  • Not enough cycling infrastructure affects the safety of cyclists or daily delivery services by forcing them to take the same routes as cars on main roads. Without well-designed cycling spaces, many incidents can accure.
